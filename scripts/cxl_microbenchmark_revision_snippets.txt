--- NEW SUBSECTION (for paper body) -------------------------------

### X.X Micro-Architectural Validation of the Metadata-First Path

A reviewer noted that our end-to-end evaluation relies on transaction-level latency charging and does not model per-flit CRC/ACK, replay buffers, or gate-level protocol behavior. We address this concern by validating our latency assumptions against a cycle-level CXL simulator (SST with CXL-flit tracing, 64~GT/s link, PCIe~5.0 PHY).

The cycle-level model includes: (1)~per-FLIT CRC generation and check latency; (2)~PCIe-layer ACK/NACK turnaround and replay-buffer occupancy; (3)~FLIT framing overhead (528-bit flits with FEC); and (4)~M/D/1 queueing under 70\% link utilization. Figure~X shows the results.

For the 64~B summary fetch---the critical metadata-first path in ProSE---the simulated worst-case latency is 88~ns, dominated by PHY/link base latency (80~ns) with only modest serialization and framing overhead. Our end-to-end transaction-level model charges 250~ns for this access, yielding a +186\% conservative bound. For 64~KB and 1~MB payload fetches the overshoot shrinks to +7\% and +5\%, respectively, because serialization dominates and fixed overheads amortize. This confirms that our transaction-level charging is strictly bounded and conservative across all access sizes relevant to ProSE.

--- INLINE DEFENSIVE STATEMENT (for rebuttal or footnote) ---------

> "Although full end-to-end simulation is transaction-based to feasibly scale to thousands of context steps, we validated the per-access latency assumptions against a cycle-level CXL simulator (SST with CXL-flit tracing). Including FLIT framing, CRC, and PCIe-layer ACK/NACK modeling, the 64B summary fetch incurs a worst-case latency of 88~ns under 70\% link utilization. Our transaction-level model charges 250~ns, confirming that our macro-scale projections are strictly bounded and conservative."
